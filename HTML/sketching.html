TRY IT YOURSELF!

Below are examples of different 'brushes' made with code. These code snippets have been taken from 'Super Libary's tutorial 'How to Paint with Code'. Refresh to clear the drawings. Have fun!

Regular Pen

The first pen is the most simple version of a brush, that draws a line from the point the mouse previously was to the point the mouse currently is, over and over again within a second. Assuming a high enough frame rate, this creates a relatively smooth drawing experience.

Marker

The marker is created by drawing many transparent circles over the top of one another. This creates a marker or felt tip like effect.

Beads

The beads are based on the speed of the mouse, so the quicker you move your mouse, or paintbrush, the bigger the circle, or stroke.

Rainbow Beads

This adds in unqiue colours to the circles, that combine to create a rainbow pattern. This technique can be used to trick the eye into thinking there is a gradient, when its actually just colours next to each other.

Wiggle

The wiggle brush uses angles and fractions of a circle to create a wiggle effect. After each semi circle is marked as complete, it is switched to be the opposite direction.

Toothpick

The toothpick brush is an extension or improvement on the traditional bruhs, adding texture using tangents.

Fountain Pen

This is my personal favourite as it most closely resemebles the real markers I use to sketch in my physical sketchbooks. I think it is cool that the direction can dictate the brush thickness.

Splatter

The splatter is a more broad application brush, with high randomness. I like that when you move quickly you can smear or splat the paint quite far off the line of the mouse itself.

Spray Paint

The spray paint code is definitely the most complicated, with the speed of mouse moving, circle radius and distribution of paint to take into account. The final result is really cool though, and could be used in many different applications.
