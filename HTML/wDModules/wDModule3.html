Module 3: Paper Prototyping

Crazy 8's (In-Class): First Four

These first four crazy 8's I really tried to think creatively from the get-go. There are two main themes, of playful shapes, as well as longer rectangles The circles show a collapsable menu where more circle come off the main one during hover. The bottom right design is inspired by playschool and their shaped windows, and when you click a module you would navigate 'through' that shape into to the page's content.

Crazy 8's (In-Class): Last Four

Once I got to the second four of the crazy-8s in class, I had a mix of more structured and unstructed. I really like the idea of incorporating a natural element, as shown by the cat and tree and maybe using animals as buttons for the modules. My style of art is quite line-work based so I feel that could be fun. In layout out information, however, I like to be quite structured so that important information is clear at first glance. Hence, the more structured ideas with the modular layouts of images and text e.g. top right.

Crazy 8's (At Home): First Four

I decided to have another try at some more crazy-8s to expand my possible ideas. For these I ended up going more theme-based. These include a chatterbox, garden, scrabble theme, and a horizontal navigation system inspired from some of my research.

Crazy 8's (At Home): Last Four

The final four of my at-home crazy-8s were themed around mushrooms, a fantasy quest, photobooth and a sheppard with sheep on a hill. These are just some of the theming ideas I had for my website, but I became slightly overwhelmed with the sheer amount of possibility. Instead, I decided to intially markup all the content for the module pages first, and then have the opportunity to tie this intp a theme later on.
